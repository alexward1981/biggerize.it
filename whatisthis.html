Biggerize.it

All biggerize.it does is make text biggerer. It can be useful in the following scenarios:

Scenario One: Leaving a clear message
It's lunchtime, you're sat playing Flappy Bird on your phone when a colleague comes over to ask you a question, they don't know you are on lunch and you have to look up and engage then like a human being to tell them that you are in fact on lunch.

The solution!
Scenario Two: Phone badgering
Your girlfriend is on the phone with your mother (which is scary enough!) and you hear her suggest she should come and stay for a weekend. You know that for the next two weekends, you both plan on watching Game of Thrones, whilst sat on the sofa in your underpants (how a sofa got in your underpants, I have no idea) so somehow you have to communicate to her that visiting in May is out of the question, without alterting your mother that you are nearby and able to speak to her on the phone.

The solution!
Scenario Three: Talking to someone across a room
There is a new girl in the office and by heck she's a looker, she might even be 'the one!'. You need to tell your mate to look but she's standing too close for you to be able to say anything out loud!

The solution!
Scenario Four: Being obnoxious
You love twitter, you send at least three hundred thousand tweets a day but you hate that if you want to send a shouty tweet then your only options are capital letters. There is no conveient way to properly get across the emotion of how smug you are that your diet is going really well (They don't need to know you're actually eating a cheeseburger).

The solution!
Scenario Five: The Gentleman from Buffy the Vampire Slayer turn out to be real and come to your town.
Oh no! Joss Whedon was surprisingly presient and predicited the arrival of demons who can steal everyones voices. Even worse, the dude in front of you totally has one of them behind him and it looks like he's gonna get stabbed. You need to let him know but you don't have a voice!

The solution!
